## 📌 Overview

A comprehensive data analysis of COVID-19's impact on India, examining epidemiological trends, vaccination rollout, healthcare infrastructure, and socio-economic factors. The project combines data cleaning in Excel with interactive Power BI dashboards to deliver actionable insights for public health decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

| Focus Area | What Was Analyzed |
|---|---|
| 📈 **Epidemiological Trends** | Cases, recoveries, and fatalities across years |
| 💉 **Vaccination Coverage** | Doses administered, population coverage, full vs partial vaccination |
| 🏥 **Healthcare Infrastructure** | Hospital bed availability and handwashing facility access |
| 💰 **Socio-economic Factors** | GDP per capita, smoking prevalence, demographic trends |
| 📊 **Human Development Index** | Correlation between HDI and COVID-19 outcomes |

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 📈 Epidemiological Metrics
- **2022 recorded the highest COVID-19 case burden** — 15.79 billion cumulative cases, representing a **435.51% increase** over 2023 figures
- Clear wave patterns visible in year-over-year case trend analysis

### 💉 Vaccination Metrics
- **2022 also peaked in vaccination** — 354 billion cumulative doses administered
- Strong positive correlation identified between total vaccinated and fully vaccinated populations, indicating high completion rates

### 🏥 Healthcare Infrastructure
- Handwashing facility access peaked in **2021–2022** at 21,735.75 cumulative units
- Notable **drop in 2023** (3,930.30) — flagged as a potential data gap or policy shift requiring further investigation
- Hospital bed availability analyzed against case surge periods

### 📉 Socio-economic Correlation
- GDP per capita and HDI showed measurable correlation with vaccination uptake
- Smoking prevalence explored as a comorbidity risk factor across states

## 💡 Recommendations

Based on the analysis, key recommendations for policymakers include:

- Sustain vaccination infrastructure beyond peak periods to maintain coverage continuity
- Address the sharp drop in handwashing facility data post-2022 — either through infrastructure investment or improved data collection
- Target lower-HDI regions for prioritized public health resource allocation
- Use GDP-vaccination correlation data to design more equitable distribution policies
